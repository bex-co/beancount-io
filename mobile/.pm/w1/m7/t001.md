## Objective

Let other screens open the Reports tab on a specific report: the reports route accepts an initial segment (spending | income | cashflow | networth) and an optional time range as route params, and the segment switcher and range state honor them on navigation — including when the tab is already mounted.

## Context

- The Reports shell (segment switcher + shared time range) ships in w1/m3/t002 at `app/(app)/(tabs)/reports.tsx` + `src/screens/reports-screen/`.
- expo-router passes params to tab routes via `router.navigate`/`Link` `params`; re-navigation to a mounted tab must re-apply the param (use `useLocalSearchParams` + effect, not just initial state).

## Steps

1. Define the accepted params (segment, optional range) and parse/validate them in the reports screen shell.
2. Apply the params to the segment switcher and time-range state on mount **and** on param change while mounted.
3. Fall back to the default segment when params are absent or invalid.

## Files

- `app/(app)/(tabs)/reports.tsx`
- `src/screens/reports-screen/` (shell/switcher from m3)

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] `router.navigate("/reports", { params: { segment: "spending" } })` (or equivalent) lands on the Spending report, including when Reports was already the mounted tab showing another segment.
- [ ] Invalid/absent params fall back to the default segment without crashing.

- Wiring the Home cards (t002–t003).
